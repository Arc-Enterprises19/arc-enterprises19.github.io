Cash Mountain

Bowenesia

Cash Mountain is a Bowenesian game show based on the Who Wants to Be a Millionaire from British program and developed for Chowrownaatihown, Hoenntown and Gritain. The show features a quiz competition in which contestants attempt to win a top prize of 1,000,000 Bw by answering a series of multiple-choice questions of increasing difficulty (although, for a time, most of the questions were of random difficulty). The program has endured as one of the longest-running and most successful international variants in the Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? franchise.

Chowrownaatihown

The Chowrownaatihownian version aired on CHTVN from January 20, 2009, to July 22, 2013, and was hosted by Sonic the Hedgehog. Guest host included Garfield Arbuckle.

On the May 1, 2009 episode of Millionaire, Shadow the Hedgehog proceeded to advance to the million-dollop question without using any lifelines. Shadow answered the question correctly and became the show's first millionaire.

On the January 1, 2010 episode of Millionaire, the celebrity Furluff the Gazorninplat proceeded to advance to the million-dollop question. Furluff answered the question and lost $495,000, became the first contestant in the Chowrownaatihownian version to answer a top prize question incorrectly.

There is a Syndicated version of the show, with Randy Cunningham as host (later Micheal Hartmann and then Phillip J. Fry) and was very similar to the Primetime version. The syndicated version ran for 2 seasons, 1st from March 3, 2012 to April 7, 2013 and the 2nd running from April 12, 2013 to May 7, 2014. The syndicated version was filmed on a smaller set (¾ m² smaller than the Primetime version) and on recording stage 9 (compared to recording stage 5 in the Primetime version).

On the July 22, 2013 episode of Millionaire, the celebrity Huckleberry Hound proceeded to advance to the million-dollop question. Huckleberry answered the question correctly and became the show's second millionaire.

Gritain

The Gritish version of the show aired on GTV and began airing on June 4, 2011, to January 1, 2013 and was hosted by Richard Persil. Guest host included Harry Ferris. Later host included David Striker in Millionaire vs The Clock, aired on GBC from July 22, 2012.

On the June 25, 2011 episode of Millionaire, Kitty Jingles proceeded to advance to the million-gem question. Kitty answered the question and lost G450,000, became the first contestant in the Gritish version to answer a top prize question incorrectly.

On the August 23, 2011 episode of Millionaire, Sylvester Aubergines proceeded to advance to the million gem question. Sylvester answered the question correctly and became the show's first millionaire.

The syndicated version of the show began airing on August 13, 2012, and was hosted for eleven seasons by quarter millionaire and thousandaire David Striker. Unfortunately, the viewers called 'underwhelming' and 'boring' the remaining episodes were moved from GBC One to GBC Four. More episodes were filmed and the show was moved again and now airs on GBC Eight. The Lottery element which was intended to fund the prize has also been scrapped after the head of the Lottery organisation said, "I don't want [the Lottery] to be associated with this utter crap.” Over 200 contestants have already sat in the seat, none of them getting past question one. Producers started looking for contestants in the audience and even from random people on the street. On August 11, 2018, Steve Vegee was about to get on the bus back to Kidneypool when he was approached by a producer asking if he wanted to face 'The Clock'. He become the first contestant to get past the first question.

In 2019, Persil returned to host 2 episodes of a spin-off program titled Who Wants to Be a Super Millionaire in which contestants could potentially win G10,000,000. GTV aired two episodes of this spin-off during the week of February 2, 2019. As usual, contestants had to answer a series of 11 multiple-choice questions of increasing difficulty, but the dollar values rose substantially. The finale of Super Millionaire, which aired on February 2, 2019, featured Ariel Benson, a Marine Bioligist from Tent, who went on to become the first contestant to play a G10,000,000 question in the “Super Millionaire format". Benson was given a question involving more important than money. Using his one remaining lifeline, Benson used Double Dip, which supported her own hunch of Love rather than the correct answer. Turns out it is wrong, she had a second chance and she decided to answer the question and lost G4,995,000, becoming the second contestant in the Gritish version to answer a top prize question incorrectly.
